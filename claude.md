# UK Tax System

This file gives you a simple overview of the UK tax system.

## Income Tax

You pay Income Tax on money you earn from: - employment\
- self employment\
- pensions\
- rental income\
- savings and investments

Rates for England and Northern Ireland: - Basic rate 20 percent\
- Higher rate 40 percent\
- Additional rate 45 percent

Scotland uses different bands.

## Personal Allowance

Most people have a tax free Personal Allowance of 12,570 GBP.\
Your allowance drops once your income passes 100,000 GBP.

## National Insurance

You pay National Insurance if you work. It funds state benefits.\
Rates depend on employment type and income band.

## Capital Gains Tax

You pay tax on profits when you sell assets like shares or property.\
Rates vary based on asset type and your Income Tax band.

## VAT

Value Added Tax is added to most goods and services.\
Standard rate is 20 percent, with reduced rates at 5 percent and zero
percent.

## Corporation Tax

Companies pay Corporation Tax on profits.\
Current main rate is 25 percent, with a small profits rate for lower
profit levels.

## Council Tax

Local authorities charge Council Tax based on property band.

## Stamp Duty

Stamp Duty applies to property purchases. Rates depend on price and
buyer status.
